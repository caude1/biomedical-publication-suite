# Publication Surface Standard

## Reader-First Rule

Write for a cold reviewer who knows the field but has not seen the chat, repository, analysis history, figure experiments, rejected approaches, or author TODOs. Every publication-facing sentence must stand on its own.

## Publication-Facing Firewall

Keep these out of manuscripts, abstracts, display text, captions, legends, footnotes, supplements, cover letters, and reviewer responses:

- repository names and file paths;
- skill names and agent workflow terms;
- changed, updated, revised, selected, demoted, elevated, or final model language when it narrates process rather than methods;
- draft, version, package, workflow, or project-management framing;
- TBD, pending, placeholder, authors will confirm, or to be added;
- figure-design rationale such as opacity, workaround, or visual de-emphasis unless stated as a formal legend key;
- apologies, excuses, or memo-style explanations.

Disclose analysis-plan changes only when protocol, peer review, or scientific transparency requires it.

## Artifact Surface Hierarchy

- Title: state what the figure, table, section, or artifact shows.
- Subtitle: add cohort, sample size, time point, data source, or scale.
- Axis label: name variable and unit.
- Panel label: identify panel-specific comparison, subgroup, time point, or outcome.
- Legend key: map encodings to categories, thresholds, uncertainty, or statistical features.
- Caption: explain how to read the display, including models, intervals, groups, panels, and encodings.
- Footnote: define abbreviations, restrictions, sparse-cell thresholds, suppression rules, and special statistical details.
- Results text: report findings and inferential uncertainty.
- Discussion text: interpret findings in proportion to evidence.
- Control surface or Notes: hold author-facing concerns and missing details.

Labels identify. They do not explain, defend, apologize, or anticipate objections.

## Sparse Data

Use exact counts, intervals, thresholds, and suppression rules. If visual encoding reflects sample size or uncertainty, define it precisely:

- Lighter fill indicates strata with n < 20.
- Dashed lines indicate estimates based on fewer than 20 events.
- Cells with fewer than 10 patients were suppressed.

Avoid vague few patients, unexplained opacity, or title-level caveats.

## Missing Information

Do not invent administrative details. If a required detail is missing:

- omit it if the artifact can function without it;
- use a bracketed placeholder only when essential;
- log it in open items;
- ask the user only when it blocks the task.

## Patient Privacy

Do not place identifiable patient information in case reports, examples, reviewer responses, cover letters, or supplemental narratives unless the user has explicitly provided publication-approved wording. Remove or generalize exact dates, rare location details, unusual occupation details, linked family details, and other indirect identifiers when they are not essential to the scientific point. Keep de-identification concerns in control notes, not in polished prose.

## Causal Discipline

Match verbs to design:

- randomized primary analysis: cautious causal language may be appropriate if the estimand supports it;
- observational/nonrandomized: association language;
- diagnostic: performance language;
- prediction: discrimination, calibration, transportability, and overfitting language;
- translational/preclinical: mechanistic or model-system language, not human clinical benefit unless shown.

Avoid for observational/nonexperimental work:

- caused;
- drove;
- led to;
- resulted in;
- due to;
- because of when it implies direction;
- mediated;
- explained;
- established;
- proved;
- demonstrated in the strong causal sense.

## Tone

Use exact, conventional biomedical language. Prefer ordinary scientific nouns and direct verbs. Avoid generic importance claims and inflated adjectives. If a finding matters, show why using the result, design, and clinical or mechanistic context.

Avoid AI-like filler such as delve, landscape, tapestry, holistic, multifaceted when used as padding, pivotal, unlock, harness, seamless, shed light, and deep dive.

## Silent Final Audit

Before returning publication-facing text, check:

- Does every claim have evidence or a citation path?
- Are all numbers preserved exactly?
- Are causal verbs justified?
- Are caveats in the right location?
- Are titles, labels, and legends doing their own jobs?
- Did internal process language leak into the artifact?
- Did confidence become overstatement?
- Did caution become mush?
