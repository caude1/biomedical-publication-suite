# Revision Response and Submission

## Revision Response Workflow

1. Parse editor and reviewer comments into a revision matrix.
2. Classify each item as critical, major, or minor.
3. Decide action: revise, add analysis, clarify, add limitation, decline with rationale, or author decision needed.
4. Make manuscript changes before drafting the final response text when possible.
5. Draft response text that names the change and location.
6. Update claim register and QC.

## Response Tone

Be calm, specific, and nondefensive. Do not overthank, overexplain, or narrate internal struggle.

Useful response structure:

1. We thank the reviewer for this comment.
2. We have revised [section] to [specific change].
3. The revised text now states: [quote or concise paraphrase].
4. If disagreeing: We agree that [concern] is important; however, [reason]. We have clarified this limitation in [section].

## Submission Readiness

A package is not submission-ready until:

- abstract, Results, tables, figures, and claim register agree;
- journal requirements are known or open items are explicit;
- reporting guideline checklist is at least partially filled;
- key citations are real and support the claims they are attached to;
- limitations name real design threats;
- no publication-facing text leaks internal process;
- unresolved author checks are quarantined in open items.

## Final Handoff

Report:

- artifacts created or revised;
- QC status;
- remaining author checks;
- whether the package is ready for submission, author review, journal adaptation, or another analysis pass.
